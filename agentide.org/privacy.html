Privacy Policy

Your privacy is fundamental to our mission of building a trustworthy, open-source ecosystem.

Last Updated: [Date]

This Privacy Policy applies to the Agent IDE Org non-profit organization ("us", "we", or "our"), our open-source software, and our managed cloud services (the "Services").

1. Self-Hosted Open-Source Software

Our core IDE is open-source software that you can run on your own infrastructure. When you use the self-hosted version, we do not collect any data from your instance. All your code, agent data, and configurations remain entirely within your control on your own servers. We have no access to it.

2. Agent IDE Cloud Service

When you use our managed cloud service, we collect the following information:

  • Account Information: Your name, email address, and organization details used to create and manage your account.
  • Usage Metadata: We collect high-level metadata to improve our service, such as the number of agents deployed, service uptime, and feature usage frequency. We do not access the specific code or proprietary data within your agents.
  • Agent Monitoring Data: For agents you deploy using our suite, we collect performance metrics (e.g., CPU usage, response time, cost) that are displayed on your monitoring dashboard. This data is treated as confidential and is not used for any other purpose.

3. Data Use and Confidentiality

Your agent's code, data, and logic are your intellectual property. We will never use your private agent data to train our own models or share it with third parties. Data collected from the cloud service is used solely to provide and improve the service, for billing, and to ensure security and performance.

4. Contributor Privacy

If you contribute to our open-source project on platforms like GitHub, your contributions, username, and other public profile information will be publicly visible, in line with the standard practices of open-source development.

5. Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect the data on our cloud platform from unauthorized access, alteration, or destruction. This includes encryption in transit and at rest, access controls, and regular security audits.
