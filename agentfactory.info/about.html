Our Vision, Mission & Principles

Building the foundational knowledge infrastructure for a responsible AI future.

Our Vision: An Informed, Responsible, and Innovative AI Future

At Agent Factory Info, we envision a future where the development and adoption of AI agent technology are driven by shared knowledge, ethical principles, and a deep understanding of its potential. We believe that for AI to benefit society, its creators and adopters must be well-informed.

Our Mission: To Centralize and Democratize AI Agent Knowledge

Our mission is to aggregate, curate, and disseminate the worldâ€™s most reliable information and educational resources on AI agents. We achieve this by curating quality content, organizing complexity, and fostering education through clear learning paths. We bridge the gap between academia, industry, and the public.

Our Story

Agent Factory Info was founded in 2024 by a team of AI researchers, technical writers, and educators frustrated by the state of public knowledge. We decided to build the solution we wished we had: a single, trustworthy website to serve as the "library of record" for the AI agent industry. We chose the .info domain because our purpose is, and always will be, to inform.

What Makes Agent Factory Info Unique?

  • Unbiased and Authoritative: As a vendor-neutral platform, our primary goal is to provide accurate information, not promote a specific product.
  • Expert Curation: Our team of subject matter experts and editors vets our content to ensure it meets the highest standards of quality and reliability.
  • Comprehensive from A to Z: We cover the entire ecosystem, from underlying technology and development frameworks to business applications and ethics.
  • Structured for Learning: We are educators at heart. Our content is organized into clear learning paths designed to take a user from novice to expert.

Our Team

Our team is a unique blend of experts united by a passion for education and technology. It includes an editorial team of former tech journalists and AI researchers, curriculum developers with backgrounds in instructional design, industry analysts who monitor the market, and community managers who nurture our forums.
